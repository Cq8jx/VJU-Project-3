**2. Credits:** 03

**6. Course Objectives:**

**6.1. Overall Objectives**
This course provides advanced knowledge of the history of philosophy and Marxist-Leninist philosophy, fostering students' worldview, philosophical thinking, and the ability to apply these to the cognition and research of subjects in the natural sciences and technology, as well as to other practical issues. Concurrently, it aims to strengthen theoretical understanding of the Vietnamese revolutionary path, particularly the science and technology development strategy of Vietnam during the period of renewal and integration.

**6.2. Specific Objectives**
-   **Knowledge:** Analyze the fundamental content of Eastern and Western philosophical history, Vietnamese philosophical thought, Marxist-Leninist philosophy, and their application in the transitional and renewal periods and in the scientific and technological development of contemporary Vietnam.
-   **Skills:** Apply philosophical theoretical thinking to research, teaching, and practical activities in the fields of natural sciences and technology.
-   **Attitude:** Participate in evaluating and critiquing research, projects, and policies related to science and technology development from the perspective of the history of philosophy and Marxist-Leninist philosophy.

**7. Learning Outcomes:**

**7.1. Knowledge**
-   K1: Be able to explain the definition, subject of study, and role of philosophy as a methodology in computer science.
-   K2: Be able to analyze the relationship between philosophy and the scientific-technological revolution, especially with artificial intelligence and high-performance computing.

**7.2. Skills**
-   S1: Be able to use philosophical critical thinking to evaluate ethical issues in AI, big data, and technology (e.g., algorithmic bias, privacy rights).
-   S2: Be able to generalize philosophical problems from the practice of software development and computer science research.

**7.3. Autonomy, Responsibility, and Ethics**
-   A1: Recognize the social responsibility of a computer scientist through philosophical principles concerning ethics and sustainable development.

**7.4. Matrix of Content and Learning Outcomes:**

| Program Learning Outcome (PLO)                 | Relevant Course Content / Objective                                | Achievement Level | Evidence of Assessment                                               |
| :--------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------- |

| PLO2: Design research topics (AI/Data Science/HPC) | S2 (Generalize philosophical problems from IT practice)            | 1/3               | Case study: Proposing a research direction based on a philosophical analysis of data ethics |
| PLO5: Propose solutions                          | A1 (Evaluate social responsibility through philosophy)             | 1/3               | A critique of a technological solution from the perspective of sustainability philosophy |

**10. Course Description (Summary of approx. 120 words per subject):**
This course presents and analyzes the characteristics of Western philosophy, Eastern philosophy (including Vietnamese philosophical thought), and Marxist philosophy. It demonstrates and clarifies the advanced content of Marxist-Leninist philosophy in the contemporary era, its worldview, and its methodological role. Through this, the course delves into the interrelationship between philosophy and various sciences, clarifying the worldview and methodological role of philosophy in scientific development, as well as the role of various sciences in social life.

**10. Course Description (Summary of approx. 120 words per subject):**
Sustainability Science is an interdisciplinary/multidisciplinary science that uses a holistic approach to address complicated issues, which have long-term impacts on the global, social, and human scales. This course covers basics of sustainability science, including key concepts, dynamics of the human and natural systems, importance and urgency of sustainability science and its relationship with existing sciences, challenges for global, social, and human systems (e.g., global warming and climate change, natural resources and energy use, environmental and biodiversity degradation, policy and governance, poverty and hunger, inequality, social conflicts, population and migration, human security), and various solutions for sustainability.
