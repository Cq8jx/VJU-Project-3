## 2. Program Objectives

### 2.1. Overall Objectives

The overall objective of this program is to cultivate high-quality human resources who are competitive in the domestic, global, and especially Japanese labor markets. This is achieved by equipping them with knowledge ranging from fundamental to modern and practical expertise in the field of Control and Automation Engineering, integrated with Artificial Intelligence and Big Data, all grounded in a liberal arts education and the capacity for lifelong learning.

### 2.2. Specific Objectives

- **Knowledge:** Students will solve problems in the field of Control and Automation based on a broad knowledge of basic sciences, sustainability science, and essential knowledge required for a 21st-century individual.
- **Skills:** Students will apply professional skills and advanced technologies in Control and Automation Engineering integrated with Artificial Intelligence and Big Data to practical work, particularly in solving interdisciplinary and cross-disciplinary challenges aimed at sustainable development. With fluent foreign language proficiency, they will operate internationally and contribute to society.
- **Attitude:** Students will develop a proactive, autonomous, creative, and professional attitude in their work, life, and lifelong learning capabilities to adapt to new trends in present and future society.

## 3. Admissions Information

Admissions will follow the regulations and guidelines on university admissions of the Ministry of Education and Training, Vietnam National University, Hanoi, and the annual university admission scheme of Vietnam Japan University approved by Vietnam National University, Hanoi.

The planned enrollment for 2025 is 100 students.

Enrollment from 2025 onwards will be determined based on practical conditions and the quota of Vietnam National University, Hanoi.

# PART II: LEARNING OUTCOMES OF THE EDUCATION PROGRAM

## 1. Knowledge-related Learning Outcomes

**Learning Outcome 1:** Correctly recognize issues related to society, politics, law, security, and national defense.

**Learning Outcome 2:** Apply knowledge of mathematics, physics, basic sciences, computational simulation, electrical engineering, control engineering, automation, measurement, and foreign languages to study and research in the field of Control and Automation Engineering.

**Learning Outcome 3:** Apply design principles and processes, technical standards, signal processing techniques, and control programming to operate automated control systems and robotic devices integrated with Artificial Intelligence and Big Data in related fields.

**Learning Outcome 4:** Analyze knowledge and technologies in the field of Control and Automation Engineering to formulate research ideas in interdisciplinary fields aimed at sustainable development.

## 2. Skill-related Learning Outcomes

**Learning Outcome 5:** Use simulation, design, and programming tools in the field of Control and Automation to install, operate, and control automated systems and devices.

**Learning Outcome 6:** Design models, systems, and application solutions within the field of Control and Automation Engineering, or develop interdisciplinary research ideas to achieve the objectives of tasks set under real-world conditions.

**Learning Outcome 7:** Clearly present ideas, listen actively, provide appropriate feedback, and solve problems flexibly.

**Learning Outcome 8:** Develop work plans and complete tasks within deadlines.

**Learning Outcome 9:** Achieve English proficiency equivalent to Level 4 of the Vietnamese 6-level Foreign Language Proficiency Framework and be able to communicate basically in Japanese.

## 3. Autonomy and Responsibility

**Learning Outcome 10:** Actively participate in group activities, demonstrate a sense of responsibility, show the ability to set personal goals, and inspire others.

**Learning Outcome 11:** Demonstrate a willingness to face change, learn proactively, and develop oneself.

## 4. Ethics

**Learning Outcome 12:** Respect cultural diversity and professional ethics, and participate in social contribution activities.

## 5. Envisioned Job Positions After Graduation

Graduates can assume positions as staff or specialists in fields such as electrical engineering, power electronics, automatic control, system design, robotics, machine learning, system analysis and design, signal processing, and automation in supply chains. They may become researchers at research institutes or R&D centers, instructors at universities, colleges, vocational schools, or technical professionals in the application of control and automation engineering in industrial, production, and socio-economic sectors within enterprises and organizations in Vietnam, Japan, and other countries.

## 6. Potential for Further Studies After Graduation

Graduates will have the capacity to pursue postgraduate programs (Master's, Ph.D.) in specialized fields related to Control and Automation Engineering at universities domestically and internationally, including in Japan, the region, and worldwide.

## Note

1.  One credit is equivalent to 50 standard student learning hours, including time for lecture attendance, guided study, self-study, research, practical experience, and participation in examinations and assessments. For in-class activities, one credit requires a minimum of 15 hours of lectures or 30 hours of practice, labs, or exercises, with one period calculated as 50 minutes.
    - (1): Lecture.
    - (2): Practice, lab, exercise, discussion, etc.
    - (3): Self-study, self-research, review for lectures, practice, internships, and taking examinations/assessments. Self-study hours are calculated by the formula: Credits × 50 - Lecture hours - Practice hours.
2.  Students must select courses according to their specialization track, based on the guidance of the Faculty of Advanced Science and Engineering. The curriculum incorporates several specialization tracks that include groups of interrelated and complementary subjects. For example:
    - For the **Signal Processing in Automation track**, students should focus on and prioritize the selection of the following courses: AET2012, AET2013, AET2016, AET2017, IEA3009, IEA3010, IEA3011, IEA3012, IEA3013, IEA3014, IEA3015, IEA3016.
    - For the **Automation in Supply Chain and Logistics track**, students should focus on and prioritize the selection of the following courses: AET2012, AET2013, AET2016, AET2017, IEA3017, IEA3018, IEA3019, IEA3020, IEA3021, IEA3022, IEA3023, IEA3024.
    - For the **Intelligent Industrial Automation track**, students should focus on and prioritize the selection of the following courses: AET2012, AET2013, AET2016, AET2017, IEA3025, IEA3026, IEA3027, IEA3028, IEA3029, IEA3030, IEA3031, IEA3032.
    - For the **Intelligent Robotic Systems with AI track**, students should focus on and prioritize the selection of the following courses: AET2012, AET2013, AET2016, AET2017, IEA3033, IEA3034, IEA3025, IEA3031, IEA3026, IEA3038, IEA3039, IEA3040, IEA3041.
    - **Students wishing to work for Japanese companies should take additional courses from the Japanese Career Orientation group**. Students should select the following elective courses: IEA1008, IEA1009, IEA1010, IEA1011, IEA1012.

Courses related to the topics of projects and the bachelor thesis are selected by students in consultation with their thesis advisor to align with their specialization track.

The subjects of Physical Education, National Defense and Security Education, and Soft Skills are not included in the total credits of the education program and are not used to calculate the semester GPA, course GPA, or cumulative GPA, but they are graduation requirements.
