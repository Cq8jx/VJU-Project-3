# GUIDANCE ON THE IMPLEMENTATION OF END-OF-MODULE EXAMINATIONS FOR THE FIRST SEMESTER, ACADEMIC YEAR 2025-2026

Pursuant to Official Dispatch No. 840/ĐHVN-ĐT dated July 30, 2025, from Vietnam Japan University on the development of the academic schedule for the 2025-2026 academic year,

Pursuant to Decision No. 1132/QĐ-ĐHVN dated October 1, 2025, from Vietnam Japan University on the promulgation of regulations concerning examination activities,

To enhance the quality of education and ensure the proper implementation of end-of-module examinations in accordance with the academic schedule, Vietnam Japan University hereby provides the following guidance to all units, faculty, staff, and students regarding the assessment of academic results and the implementation of end-of-module examinations for the first semester of the 2025-2026 academic year.

## 1. Implementation Principles

- To ensure integrity, accuracy, fairness, and transparency in the implementation of end-of-module examinations.
- To guarantee the rights of learners in the assessment of end-of-module examination results.
- To ensure that examinations can assess the achievement of the module's learning outcomes.
- To ensure the proper implementation of the academic progress and plans for the first semester of the 2025-2026 academic year at Vietnam Japan University and external institutions (including the VNU Center for Physical Education & Sports, the VNU Institute for Digital Education and Examination, and the VNU University of Languages and International Studies).

### 2.2. Examination Implementation Process

The implementation of end-of-module examinations for the first semester of the 2025-2026 academic year includes developing the examination plan and announcing the schedule, creating examination questions and grading, assigning proctors (CBCT), entering scores and announcing results, and re-grading examinations. The specific process is detailed in Appendix 2.

### 2.3. Requirements for Creating Examination Questions and Answer Keys

- End-of-module examination questions must be able to assess the module's learning outcomes (CĐR).
- Examination questions must not be identical to those from previous semester examinations, quizzes, exercises, or review questions distributed to learners in advance, even for modules where the examination papers are required to be collected.
- For modules taught in multiple classes, a common examination paper must be created. All instructors and the Program Director/Dean must agree on the requirements and basic content of the examination paper in advance (special cases require approval from the Rector's Office).
- For modules with an existing examination question bank, the Head of the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance will compile the examination papers based on the approved question bank.
- For examinations in the form of reports, assignments, or presentations, the submission deadline and method shall be proposed by the module instructor and decided through the Program Director/Dean, but must comply with the University's academic and examination schedule. The module instructor is responsible for implementing the plan, collecting and grading the reports, submitting the examination questions, scoring rubrics, reports, and presentations, and submitting the end-of-module examination grade sheet to the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance on time. Learners must follow the instructor's instructions correctly.
- For oral examinations: Each examination question must have a clear scoring rubric for each part, ensuring completeness, accuracy, comprehensiveness, applicability, uniform difficulty across questions, and appropriateness for the number of credits and the duration of the examination.
- Duration of end-of-module examinations:
    + Essay or combined essay and multiple-choice format: 30 minutes per credit, but not exceeding 120 minutes for modules of 4 or more credits.
    + Multiple-choice format: 20 minutes per credit, but not exceeding 90 minutes for modules of 4 or more credits.
    + Oral examination format: 15 minutes for preparation, and approximately 7-10 minutes for presenting the answer and responding to additional questions from the examiner. Modules requiring more than 15 minutes of preparation time must be specified in the module syllabus.
- Examination questions must be accompanied by an answer key with a scoring rubric and clear grading criteria. Grades are on a 10-point scale, marked to one decimal place. For multiple-choice tests or foreign language tests, grading may be done using a different rubric and then converted to the 10-point scale (see templates in Appendix 3 and 4).
- Examination questions and answer keys must be placed in separate envelopes, sealed, and stamped with a seal over the flap. Each envelope must be clearly labeled with the same information as the top of the examination paper, along with the name and phone number of the creator. The author of the answer key must sign and write their full name on it (in accordance with the attached template). If necessary, the University will publish the answer key to allow learners to review their own papers.
- Number of examination papers per session:
    + Essay or combined essay and multiple-choice formats: At least 2 different versions of the paper must be created for each examination session.
    + Multiple-choice format: At least 2 question codes for written tests and at least 4 question codes for computer-based tests must be provided for each question.
    + Oral examination format: Questions shall be prepared as a set (minimum of 10 questions) from which learners will draw lots to select and answer during the examination time.
- Format of end-of-module examination questions:
    + Examination questions must be typed or clearly handwritten on one side of A4 paper in black or dark blue ink, with a margin of at least 2 cm from the edge of the paper.
    + The top of the examination paper must clearly state the module name, module code, number of credits, question number, and examination duration.
    + The bottom of the examination paper must specify if learners are permitted to use materials.
    + For multiple-choice examinations, the multiple-choice answer sheet template must be attached to the examination questions (see Appendix 3c).

### 2.4. Requirements for Grading

- Assignment of graders: The Dean/Program Director is responsible for assigning instructors to grade the end-of-module examination papers, ensuring that each module is graded by at least two instructors. The grading process must be conducted with rigor, objectivity, accuracy, and fairness.
- Grading and score submission process:
    + Grading on paper (essay, combined essay and multiple-choice, and multiple-choice answer sheets):
        * The Office of Testing and Quality Assurance is responsible for preparing a room for instructors to conduct grading and for monitoring the grading progress.
        * From the day after the module's examination date, instructors assigned to grade can receive the answer scripts from the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance. Graders must submit the graded scripts and grade sheets to the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance no later than 2 weeks (10 working days) after the examination date.
        * Grading of end-of-module examination papers must follow the official answer key and scoring rubric submitted to the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance. Instructors shall grade directly on the answer scripts on a 10-point scale, to one decimal place. The scripts must be signed by two grading instructors. If errors are found, the instructor must submit a grade correction request, clearly stating the reason for the correction.
    + Grading in the computer lab:
        * For modules graded directly on the computer: After the examination time ends, learners must stop working and remain in their seats until the proctor confirms the results. The proctor will record the examination results displayed on the computer screen onto the grade sheet. Learners must double-check the results on the grade sheet before signing the attendance list. The proctor is responsible for the accuracy of the examination results recorded on the grade sheet. The grade sheet shall be made in triplicate, with the original submitted to the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance, one copy to the faculty, and one copy to the Department of Academic and Student Affairs.
        * For modules that cannot be graded directly in the examination room: The proctor is responsible for saving the learners' answer scripts to a CD or USB immediately after each examination session. Immediately after the examination, the proctor shall hand over the attendance list and the CD or USB containing the learners' answer scripts to the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance. The Office of Testing and Quality Assurance will then hand over the CD or USB containing the learners' answer scripts to the staff responsible for grading, in accordance with regulations.
        * Grading of end-of-module examination papers must follow the official answer key and scoring rubric submitted to the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance. Instructors shall grade on a grading sheet and return the CD or USB containing the answer scripts, the grading sheet, and the grade sheet to the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance no later than 2 weeks (10 working days) after the examination date.
    + Grading of presentations and reports: Grading of end-of-module examination papers must follow the official answer key and scoring rubric submitted to the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance. Instructors shall grade on a grading sheet and submit the grading sheet, answer scripts, and grade sheet to the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance no later than 2 weeks (10 working days) after the examination date.
    + Grading of oral examinations: Grading of end-of-module examination papers must follow the official answer key and scoring rubric submitted to the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance. For oral examinations where the learner's response is not recorded, the results must be announced to the learner immediately after the examination and submitted to the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance no later than 1 working day after the examination date.

### 2.5. Processing of Re-graded Examination Results

- Upon receiving a re-grading request from a learner, the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance shall immediately retrieve the examination paper to be re-graded and notify the academic unit. The Dean/Program Director will assign graders to conduct the re-grading within a maximum of 7 working days. For common modules, the regulations of Vietnam National University, Hanoi shall apply.
- The processing of re-graded results shall be conducted in the same manner as the initial grading.
- If the re-graded score differs from the initial score (already announced) by 0.5 to 1.0 point, the score shall be corrected immediately.
- If the re-graded score differs from the initial score by more than 1.0 point, the Dean/Program Director shall assign a different staff member from the initial grader to facilitate a direct dialogue between the first and second graders to agree on and finalize the re-graded score, and a record of the meeting shall be created.
- If, during the grade processing, any party discovers signs of intentional violation, they shall report it to the Audit and Legal Affairs division for handling.
- The re-graded results will be processed by the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance in accordance with regulations and sent to the Department of Academic and Student Affairs to be announced to the learner no later than 3 working days after the end of the re-grading period.

## 3. Responsibilities of Departments and Individuals

### 3.1. Office of Testing and Quality Assurance

- Consolidate information on the format of end-of-module examinations from the units coordinating the courses.
- Create the schedule for end-of-module examinations.
- Prepare the list of examinees and arrange examination rooms within the specified period.
- Announce the examination schedule to all relevant parties within the specified period.
- Create a list for the dispatch of proctors (CBCT), send it to the relevant units, and urge them to submit the list of assigned proctors within the specified period.
- Receive and store scoring rubrics, samples of graded partial assessment papers, final grade sheets, and end-of-module examination scripts. The storage method and duration shall comply with the current guidance and regulations of the Ministry of Education and Training and Vietnam National University, Hanoi.
- Receive examination questions and answer keys from instructors, and manage the examination question bank and end-of-module examination papers in both hard copy (printed version) and computer data file formats.
- Shuffle, select, print, and duplicate end-of-module examination questions in accordance with the University's processes, schedules, and regulations for examination activities, and adhere to confidentiality protocols as required by law.
- Prepare examination rooms (reserve examination rooms and waiting rooms, report to security to arrange desks and chairs according to the number of examinees on the list).
- Prepare grading rooms, hand over examination questions, answer sheets/mark sheets, and receive answer scripts from proctors. Hand over answer scripts, end-of-module examination grade sheets, and guiding documents and regulations related to grading. Urge relevant units to grade, return answer scripts, and submit grade sheets in accordance with regulations.
- Notify learners of the period and process for re-grading applications. Act as the focal point for re-grading end-of-module examinations upon request from learners.

### 3.2. Department of Academic and Student Affairs

- Supervise and urge instructors to confirm the format of final examinations for the modules coordinated by the Department of Academic and Student Affairs.
- Supervise and urge faculties, academic programs, and instructors to submit the partial assessment grade sheets for each module in each semester.
- Provide instructors with accounts for the grade entry software and handle any issues related to these accounts. Notify instructors of the deadlines for updating partial assessment grades and submitting partial assessment grade sheets on the University's current grade entry software.
- Handle issues related to partial assessment grade data that arise between students and instructors.
- Receive end-of-module examination grade data from the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance, and update the module assessment grade data on the academic management software and the academic information portal for learners' reference.

### 3.3. Other Administrative Departments

- Department of Planning and Finance: Collaborate with the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance to prepare the budget for examinations and provide the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance with a list of learners who have outstanding fees or tuition.
- Administration Office: Inspect and supervise the implementation process of examination activities based on the authority of the Audit and Legal Affairs division. Collaborate with relevant parties to advise, guide, and coordinate to ensure the smooth implementation of work processes.
- Departments and individuals in charge of classrooms, laboratories, and computer rooms are responsible for the facilities and equipment during examinations that use the facilities under their management.
- Heads of administrative departments are responsible for dispatching proctors upon request from the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance.

### 3.4. Deans/Program Directors, Co-Directors

- Disseminate the regulations concerning examination activities and the content requirements for end-of-module examinations to all faculty, staff, and learners.
- Dispatch faculty members, researchers, or faculty assistants to participate as proctors in examination supervision.
- Assign module instructors, examination question creators, and graders for modules directly managed by the faculty.

### 3.5. Instructors

- Send the list of learners not eligible to take the examination to the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance.
- Submit examination questions, answer keys/scoring rubrics, and end-of-module examination grades to the Office of Testing and Quality Assurance within the specified period.
- Enter end-of-module examination grades into the grade entry system.
- Conduct re-grading in accordance with regulations (if applicable).

### 3.6. Proctors

- Act with a high sense of responsibility and fully understand the regulations regarding examination proctoring.
- Correctly execute the end-of-module examination proctoring process as described in Appendix 5.

### 3.7. Undergraduate and Graduate Students

- Pay tuition fees on time.
- Study independently and prepare for examinations to achieve good results.
- Correctly fulfill the responsibilities of examinees in the examination room as specified in Appendix 7.
