## Guidance on the Implementation of the End-of-Module Examination for the First Semester of the 2025-2026 Academic Year

Pursuant to Official Letter No. 840/ĐHVN-ĐT of Vietnam Japan University dated July 30, 2025, on the development of the academic training schedule for the 2025-2026 academic year,

Pursuant to Decision No. 1132/QĐ-ĐHVN of Vietnam Japan University dated October 1, 2025, promulgating the Regulations on Examination Activities at Vietnam Japan University,

To improve the quality of education and to properly implement the end-of-module examinations in accordance with the academic training schedule, Vietnam Japan University hereby provides the following guidance to all departments, faculty, staff, and students regarding the assessment and implementation of the end-of-module examinations for the first semester of the 2025-2026 academic year.

### 1. Implementation Principles

- To ensure integrity, accuracy, fairness, and transparency in the implementation of end-of-module examinations.
- To guarantee the rights of learners in the assessment of end-of-module examination results.
- To ensure that the assessment requirements for the achievement of the module's learning outcomes are met.
- To ensure adherence to the progress, plans, and academic training schedules for the first semester of the 2025-2026 academic year at Vietnam Japan University and external institutions (including the VNU Center for Physical Education and Sports, the VNU Institute for Digital Education and Examination, and the VNU University of Foreign Languages).

### 2. Implementation of the End-of-Module Examination

#### 2.2. Examination Implementation Process

The implementation of the end-of-module examinations for the first semester of the 2025-2026 academic year includes developing the examination plan and announcing the schedule, creating and marking examination questions, assigning proctors (CBCT), entering grades and announcing results, and handling requests for re-marking of answer scripts. The specific process is detailed in Appendix 2.

#### 2.3. Requirements for the Creation of Examination Questions and Answer Keys

- The questions for the end-of-module examination must be able to assess the learning outcomes of the respective module.
- Examination questions must not be identical to those from previous final exams, quizzes, exercises, or review questions distributed to learners in advance, even for modules where the examination papers are to be collected.
- For modules taught in multiple classes, a common examination paper must be created, and prior agreement must be reached among all responsible instructors and the Program Director/Dean regarding the requirements and basic content of the examination questions (in special cases, approval from the Rector's Office is required).
- For modules with an existing question bank, the Head of the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance will compile the examination questions based on the approved question bank.
- For examinations in the form of essays, reports, or presentations, the submission deadline and method shall be proposed by the instructor and approved through the Program Director/Dean, but must adhere to the University's academic and examination schedule. The instructor is responsible for implementing the plan, collecting and marking the submissions, and submitting the questions, assessment criteria, essays/reports, and end-of-module examination grade sheets to the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance. Learners must follow the instructor's instructions correctly.
- For oral examinations: Each examination question must have clear scoring criteria for each part, ensuring completeness, accuracy, comprehensiveness, applicability, uniform difficulty across questions, and appropriateness for the number of credits and examination time.
- Duration of the end-of-module examination:
  - Essay-style or combined essay-style and multiple-choice: 30 minutes per credit, but not exceeding 120 minutes for modules of 4 or more credits.
  - Multiple-choice: 20 minutes per credit, but not exceeding 90 minutes for modules of 4 or more credits.
  - Oral examination: 15 minutes for preparation, and approximately 7-10 minutes for presenting the answer and responding to additional questions from the instructor. For modules requiring more than 15 minutes of preparation time, this must be specified in the detailed syllabus.
- Examination questions must be accompanied by an answer key with a marking scheme and clear assessment criteria. The marking scale is out of 10, with scores rounded to one decimal place. For multiple-choice questions and foreign language tests, a different scoring scale may be used, which is then converted to the 10-point scale (see templates in Appendix 3 and 4).
- The examination questions and answer key must be placed in separate envelopes, sealed, and stamped. Each envelope must clearly state the same information as the header of the examination paper, along with the name and phone number of the question setter. The creator of the answer key must sign and state their full name on the answer key (according to the attached template). If necessary, the University will publish the answer key to allow learners to self-check their papers.
- Number of examination papers per session:
  - Essay-style, or combined essay-style and multiple-choice: A minimum of 2 different papers per examination session.
  - Multiple-choice: A minimum of 2 different question codes for written tests and a minimum of 4 different question codes for computer-based tests.
  - Oral examination: Questions shall be prepared in a set (minimum of 10 questions) from which learners will draw lots to answer during the examination session.
- Format of the end-of-module examination paper:
  - The examination paper must be typed or clearly handwritten in black or dark blue ink on one side of A4 paper, with margins of at least 2 cm from the edge.
  - The top of the examination paper must clearly state the module name, module code, number of credits, paper number, and examination duration.
  - The bottom of the examination paper must specify if learners are permitted to use materials.
  - For multiple-choice examinations, a template of the multiple-choice answer sheet must be attached to the examination paper (see Appendix 3c).

#### 2.4. Requirements for Marking

- Assignment of markers: The Dean/Program Director is responsible for assigning instructors to mark the end-of-module examination scripts, ensuring that each module is handled by at least two instructors. The marking process must be conducted with rigor, objectivity, accuracy, and fairness.
- Marking and grade submission process
  - Marking on paper (essay-style, combined essay and multiple-choice, and multiple-choice answer sheets).
    - The Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance is responsible for preparing a room for instructors to conduct marking and for monitoring the marking progress.
    - Starting from the day after the examination date, the assigned instructors can collect the answer scripts from the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance. Markers must submit the marked scripts and grade sheets to the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance within two weeks (10 working days) of the examination date.
    - The marking of end-of-module examination scripts must follow the official answer key and marking scheme submitted to the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance. The marking instructors shall mark directly on the answer scripts on a 10-point scale, rounded to one decimal place. The script must be signed by two markers. If an error is discovered, the instructor must submit a grade correction request, clearly stating the reason for the correction.
  - Marking in a computer lab
    - For modules marked directly on the computer: After the examination time ends, learners must stop their work and remain at their stations until the proctor confirms the results. The proctor will record the examination results displayed on the computer screen onto the grade sheet. Learners must re-verify the results on the grade sheet before signing the attendance list. The proctor is responsible for the accuracy of the scores recorded on the grade sheet. The grade sheet shall be made in triplicate, with the original kept by the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance, one copy for the Faculty, and one copy for the Department of Academic and Student Affairs.
    - For modules that cannot be marked directly in the examination room: The proctor is responsible for saving the learners' submissions to a CD or USB immediately after each examination session. Immediately after the examination, the proctor shall hand over the attendance list and the CD or USB containing the learners' submissions to the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance. The Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance will then hand over the CD or USB containing the learners' submissions to the assigned markers in accordance with regulations.
    - The marking of end-of-module examination scripts must follow the official answer key and marking scheme submitted to the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance. The marking instructors shall record scores on a marking sheet and return the CD or USB with the answer scripts, the marking sheet, and the grade sheet to the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance within two weeks (10 working days) of the examination date.
  - Marking of presentations and essays: The marking of end-of-module examination scripts must follow the official answer key and marking scheme submitted to the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance. The marking instructors shall record scores on a marking sheet and submit the marking sheet, answer scripts, and grade sheets to the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance within two weeks (10 working days) of the examination date.
  - Marking of oral examinations: The marking of end-of-module examination scripts must follow the official answer key and marking scheme submitted to the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance. For oral examination formats where the learner's response is not recorded, the examination results must be announced to the learner immediately after the examination and submitted to the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance no later than one working day after the end-of-module examination date.

#### 2.5. Processing of Re-marking Results

- Upon receiving a re-marking request from a learner, the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance shall immediately retrieve the answer script to be re-marked and notify the relevant academic unit. The Dean/Program Director will appoint markers to conduct the re-marking within a maximum of 7 working days. For common modules, the regulations of Vietnam National University, Hanoi shall apply.
- The processing of re-marking results shall be carried out in the same manner as the initial marking.
- If the re-marking result differs from the initially published score by 0.5 to 1.0 point, the score shall be immediately corrected.
- If the re-marking result differs from the initial score by more than 1.0 point, the Dean/Program Director shall appoint a different marker from the initial one and arrange a direct dialogue between the first and second markers to agree on and finalize the re-marked score. A record of this meeting shall be made.
- If any signs of intentional violation are discovered by the involved parties during the score processing, it shall be reported to the Audit and Legal Affairs Department for handling.
- The re-marking results will be processed by the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance in accordance with regulations and sent to the Department of Academic and Student Affairs for notification to the learner no later than 3 working days after the end of the re-marking period.

### 3. Responsibilities of Departments and Individuals

#### 3.1. Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance

- Consolidate information on the format of end-of-module examinations from each academic unit.
- Create the schedule for the end-of-module examinations.
- Create lists of examinees and arrange examination rooms within the specified period.
- Announce the examination schedule to all relevant parties within the specified period.
- Create a list for the assignment of proctors (CBCT), send it to the relevant departments, and urge them to submit their list of assigned personnel within the specified period.
- Receive and store assessment criteria sheets, examples of marked formative assessment scripts, final grade sheets, and end-of-module examination scripts. The storage method and duration shall comply with the current guidelines and regulations of the Ministry of Education and Training and Vietnam National University, Hanoi.
- Receive examination questions and answer keys from instructors; manage the question bank, end-of-module examination papers (printed version), and computer data files (data files).
- Shuffle, draw, print, and duplicate end-of-module examination questions in accordance with the University's examination processes, schedule, and regulations, ensuring confidentiality as prescribed by law.
- Prepare examination rooms (book examination rooms, waiting rooms, and request security to arrange desks and chairs according to the number of examinees on the list).
- Prepare marking rooms, hand over examination papers, answer sheets/multiple-choice answer sheets to proctors, and receive answer scripts. Hand over answer scripts, end-of-module examination grade sheets, and guiding documents and regulations on marking. Urge relevant departments to conduct marking, return scripts, and submit grade sheets as per regulations.
- Notify learners of the period and procedure for re-marking applications. Serve as the point of contact for re-marking of end-of-module examination scripts upon learners' requests.

#### 3.2. Department of Academic and Student Affairs

- Supervise and urge instructors to confirm the format of the final examinations for the modules managed by the Department of Academic and Student Affairs.
- Supervise and urge faculties, academic programs, and instructors to submit the formative assessment grade sheets for each module in each semester.
- Provide instructors with accounts for the grade entry software and handle issues related to these accounts. Notify instructors of the deadline for updating formative assessment grades and submitting formative assessment grade sheets on the University's current grade entry software.
- Receive and handle issues related to formative assessment grade data between students and instructors.
- Receive end-of-module examination grade data from the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance and update the module assessment grade data on the academic management software and the academic information portal for learners to check.

#### 3.3. Other Relevant Departments

- Department of Planning and Finance: Coordinate with the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance to prepare the finances for the examinations and provide the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance with a list of learners who have not paid their tuition fees.
- Office of Administration: Supervise and inspect the examination implementation process based on the functional authority of the Audit and Legal Affairs division. Coordinate with relevant departments to advise, guide, and collaborate to ensure the smooth running of the operational process.
- Departments and individuals in charge of classrooms, practice rooms, and computer labs are responsible for the physical facilities during examinations using the facilities under their management.
- Heads of relevant departments are responsible for dispatching proctors upon the request of the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance.

#### 3.4. Deans/Program Directors, Co-Directors

- Disseminate the regulations on examination activities and the content of end-of-module examination requirements to all faculty, staff, and learners.
- Assign instructors, researchers, or faculty assistants to participate in proctoring duties.
- Appoint module coordinators, question setters, and markers for modules directly managed by the faculty.

#### 3.5. Instructors

- Send the list of learners not eligible to take the examination to the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance.
- Submit examination questions, answer keys/assessment criteria, and end-of-module examination grades to the Department of Testing and 2. Quality Assurance within the specified period.
- Enter end-of-module examination grades into the grade entry system.
- Conduct re-marking according to regulations (if applicable).

#### 3.6. Proctors

- Be responsible and knowledgeable about the regulations concerning proctoring.
- Correctly execute the proctoring process for end-of-module examinations as described in Appendix 5.

#### 3.7. Undergraduate and Graduate Students

- Pay tuition fees on time.
- Proactively study and prepare for the examinations to achieve good results.
- Comply with the responsibilities of examinees in the examination room as specified in Appendix 7.
